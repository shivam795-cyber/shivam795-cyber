# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites.

In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends an email or message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message often contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website. When the user enters their login details or financial information, the attacker captures it. These details are then used for unauthorized access or financial fraud.

Students and young internet users are common targets because they may not always verify the authenticity of links or messages. The consequences of phishing can be serious, including loss of money, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links and sharing personal information online. Awareness and careful verification can help prevent such cybercrimes.
